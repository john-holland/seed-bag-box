## Germination Phases

### Phase 1: Germinating (Days 0-7)
- Seed is in growing medium
- Monitoring for radicle emergence
- **Status**: Not ready for shipment

### Phase 2: Radicle (Days 5-10)
- Root has emerged from seed
- Beginning to establish
- **Status**: Not ready for shipment

### Phase 3: Cotyledon (Days 7-14)
- First leaves (cotyledons) visible
- Stem elongating
- Root system developing
- **Status**: Approaching shipment readiness

### Phase 4: True Leaf (Days 10-21)
- True leaves emerging beyond cotyledons
- Stronger root system
- Plant more resilient
- **Status**: Check shipment criteria

### Phase 5: Ready for Shipment (Days 14-28)
- Meets all shipment criteria:
  - âœ… Minimum root length
  - âœ… Minimum shoot length
  - âœ… Minimum leaf count
  - âœ… Good health status
- **Status**: Can be shipped immediately

## Shipment Types

### 1. Live Sprout with Growing Medium
**Description**: Sprout remains in original growing medium (rockwool, peat pellet, etc.)

**Advantages**:
- No transplant shock
- Roots protected
- Moisture retained
- Easy customer transition

**Packaging**:
- Individual cells or trays
- Wrapped in moist paper towel
- Placed in ventilated container
- Temperature control if needed

**Best for**: Most species, beginner customers

### 2. Bare Root
**Description**: Sprout removed from medium, roots cleaned and protected

**Advantages**:
- Lighter shipping weight
- Lower cost
- Customer can choose their medium
- Better for hydroponic customers

**Packaging**:
- Roots wrapped in moist paper
- Sealed in plastic bag
- Protected from crushing
- Must ship quickly

**Best for**: Experienced growers, hydroponic setups

### 3. Potted
**Description**: Sprout transplanted to small pot with soil

**Advantages**:
- Most established
- Easiest for customer
- Better survival rate
- Premium option

**Packaging**:
- 2-4 inch pots
- Secured to prevent tipping
- Drainage holes covered
- Heavy but stable

**Best for**: Premium customers, delicate species

### 4. Microgreens (Cut)
**Description**: Young shoots harvested and packed for eating

**Advantages**:
- Edible immediately
- No growing required
- Highly nutritious
- Fast turnaround

**Packaging**:
- Clamshell containers
- Moisture controlled
- Breathable film
- Refrigerated shipping

**Best for**: Culinary customers, restaurants

## Packaging Requirements

### Container Types

#### Ventilated Box
- Cardboard with ventilation holes
- Size: 6" x 6" x 6" for 4-6 sprouts
- Lined with moisture-resistant material
- **Use for**: Most shipments

#### Plastic Container with Lid
- Clear or opaque plastic
- Secure snap lid with air holes
- Reusable by customer
- **Use for**: Premium shipments, delicate species

#### Padded Envelope
- For microgreens only
- Contains clamshell or bag
- Insulated if needed
- **Use for**: Cut greens, small quantities

### Moisture Management

**For Live Plants**:
1. Wrap growing medium or roots in moist (not wet) paper towel
2. Place in sealed plastic bag with small air holes
3. Avoid excess water (causes mold)
4. Monitor humidity inside package

**For Microgreens**:
1. Lightly mist before packaging
2. Use breathable container
3. Include moisture-absorbing packet if needed
4. Keep refrigerated

## Customer Preference Management

### Collecting Preferences

When customer signs up or updates profile:
- **Preferred species**: What they want to grow
- **Excluded species**: Allergies or preferences
- **Experience level**: Beginner, intermediate, advanced
- **Growing setup**: Indoor, outdoor, greenhouse, hydroponic
- **Has grow lights**: Yes/No
- **Delivery frequency**: Weekly, biweekly, monthly, as-ready
- **Preferred delivery days**: To ensure they're home
- **Quantity per shipment**: Min/max sprouts

### Matching Plants to Customers

**Beginner Customers**:
- Send easy species (basil, lettuce, tomatoes)
- Include detailed instructions
- Potted shipments preferred
- Avoid finicky species

**Advanced Customers**:
- Can handle any species
- Bare root acceptable
- Minimal instructions needed
- Experimental varieties OK

**Indoor Growers**:
- Prioritize low-light species
- Smaller varieties
- Container-friendly plants

**Hydroponic Setups**:
- Bare root preferred
- Species that work well hydroponically
- Rockwool or similar medium

## Seasonal Considerations

### Spring (Mar-May)
- **High demand**: Peak planting season
- **Species focus**: Tomatoes, peppers, herbs, lettuce
- **Watch**: Late frosts, ship after last frost date
- **Opportunity**: Maximum volume

### Summer (Jun-Aug)
- **Moderate demand**: Some outdoor planting
- **Species focus**: Succession crops, fall crops
- **Watch**: Heat, require temperature control
- **Opportunity**: Indoor gardeners, fall planning

### Fall (Sep-Nov)
- **Moderate demand**: Fall gardens, indoor setups
- **Species focus**: Greens, herbs, cold-tolerant crops
- **Watch**: Shorter days, cold snaps
- **Opportunity**: Indoor gardening surge

### Winter (Dec-Feb)
- **Lower demand**: Primarily indoor
- **Species focus**: Microgreens, herbs, indoor varieties
- **Watch**: Freezing temperatures, holiday delays
- **Opportunity**: Grow lights sales, indoor gardening
