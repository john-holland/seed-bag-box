## Common Foodborne Pathogens & Contamination Sources

### Bacterial Contamination

#### Salmonella
- **Risk Level**: HIGH
- **Sources**: Raw produce, contaminated water, animal contact
- **Recent Outbreaks**: 
  - Onions (2023-2024)
  - Cantaloupe (2023)
  - Ground beef (2024)
- **Prevention**: 
  - Proper washing and sanitization of produce
  - Temperature control
  - Cross-contamination prevention
  - Water quality testing

#### E. coli (including O157:H7)
- **Risk Level**: HIGH
- **Sources**: Contaminated water, soil, animal feces
- **Recent Outbreaks**:
  - Lettuce/leafy greens (recurring)
  - Sprouts (high risk)
- **Prevention**:
  - Water source verification
  - Soil testing
  - Separation from animal operations
  - Thorough washing protocols

#### Listeria monocytogenes
- **Risk Level**: HIGH (can be fatal)
- **Sources**: Soil, water, processing environments
- **Common in**: Ready-to-eat produce, sprouts
- **Prevention**:
  - Environmental monitoring
  - Refrigeration
  - Sanitation of surfaces and equipment

### Fungal/Mold Contamination

- **Aspergillus** (produces aflatoxins)
- **Fusarium** (produces mycotoxins)
- **Botrytis** (gray mold)
- **Prevention**:
  - Humidity control
  - Air circulation
  - Regular inspection
  - Proper storage conditions

### Chemical Contamination

- **Pesticide residues**
- **Heavy metals** (arsenic, lead, cadmium)
- **Cleaning chemical residues**
- **Prevention**:
  - Testing protocols
  - Organic growing practices
  - Proper cleaning procedures
  - Documentation and traceability

## Contamination Prevention Protocols

### Spatial Quarantine Requirements
1. **New Plant Quarantine**
   - Minimum 14-day isolation
   - Visual inspection daily
   - Testing before introduction to main greenhouse

2. **Disease Detection Response**
   - Immediate isolation of affected plants
   - 3-5 meter minimum distance from healthy plants
   - Air circulation considerations
   - Tool sterilization between zones

3. **Phenotype Isolation** (for cannabis)
   - Separate ventilation zones
   - Minimum distance based on flowering type
   - Prevent cross-pollination

### Sanitation Protocols

#### Bag Cleaning Process
- Initial inspection and sorting
- Removal of organic matter
- Washing with approved sanitizers
- Drying to prevent mold growth
- Final inspection
- Quarantine period before reuse

#### Equipment Sanitation
- Daily cleaning of tools and surfaces
- Use of EPA-registered sanitizers
- Rotation of equipment between zones
- Sterilization protocols for high-risk activities
